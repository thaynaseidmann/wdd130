Main Fork of the Salmon River, Idaho

The Main Fork of the Salmon River in Idaho, located within the Frank Church Wilderness of No Return, is often referred to as the "River of No Return" due to its breathtaking rugged beauty that captivates visitors. This remote area is home to diverse wildlife, including herds of elk and deer, as well as frequent sightings of bears and bighorn sheep. The river offers a range of rapids from class I to IV, making it a popular destination for whitewater enthusiasts. The Salmon River is one of the longest undammed rivers in the United States, with water levels varying from higher and faster in early summer to lower and slower in late summer. Families with children who enjoy swimming often prefer the late summer season for its warmer and calmer waters.

Desolation and Gray Canyon, Utah

Desolation Canyon in central Utah is a remarkable and desolate landscape through which the Green River winds its way toward the mighty Colorado River and the sea. This 84-mile journey, stretching from Sand Wash to Swaseys Rapid just outside Green River, Utah, offers stunning high grey stone cliffs, captivating desert scenery, and thrilling rapids that have earned it a well-deserved reputation. The canyon is rated as a Class II/III whitewater destination, typically completed in about six days. Desolation Canyon is designated as a National Landmark due to its exceptional uniqueness, rich history, and the compelling story it tells through its geologic formations and evidence of prehistoric and historic human activity..
